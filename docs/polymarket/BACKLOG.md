## Why this domain is different (the one real advantage)

**Prediction markets RESOLVE, so every resolved market is a free ground-truth label.** Unlike the
BTC price-forecasting program (where the future is unknowable and every backtest is an upper
bound), here calibration, wallet-skill persistence, and arbitrage claims are **cleanly decidable
from free resolved data**. That is what makes the owner's two requirements — *$0 data* and
*provable results* — simultaneously achievable.

## Full backlog by family (35 hypotheses)

### Calibration / favorite-longshot

**PM01 — Extreme-longshot (<=5c) over-pricing: short the tail net of spread+gas**  
*prior:* worth-testing (critique: -) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 5/5  
- **Claim:** Resolved binary markets priced <=0.05 YES at a fixed pre-resolution lead (T-7d/T-24h) resolve YES strictly LESS often than price implies (favorite-longshot tail over-pricing), and the calibration gap exceeds the round-trip spread+gas you must cross to short (buy NO) the longshot.
- **Mechanism:** Classic favorite-longshot bias (Thaler-Ziemba, Snowberg-Wolfers): retail overweights small probabilities (prospect-theory probability weighting, lottery/skew preference, 1-2c min-tick anchoring), so a 4c contract that pays ~2% of the time leaves a seller's edge. On Polymarket the trade is buying NO on the over-priced longshot. Round-number clustering at 1/2/5c adds a sticky floor of over-priced tails.
- **Trade:** At a fixed pre-resolution lead, for every market with YES mid in (0,0.05], buy NO (sell YES) one unit; hold to resolution; PnL per unit = (1-outcomeYES)-(1-price) minus the FULL round-trip spread on the 95-99c NO notional + gas. Pre-register ONE band+lead, not the argmax. Round-number sub-test: compare exactly-on-round (1/2/3/5c) vs off-round (3.4c) tails.
- **Ground truth:** Every market resolves: outcomePrices=[1,0]/[0,1] is the label. Bucket all <=5c markets, compare empirical YES frequency to mean price with binomial CIs — fully model-free from the resolved set. The existing scripts/campaign-D/calib_gauntlet.ts + fetch_resolved.mjs already build exactly this curve.
- **Right null:** Calibrated-Bernoulli surrogate: resample each market's outcome ~ Bernoulli(price_i) keeping prices+strategy fixed; take the MAX statistic over the full (direction x band x lead) grid per draw => family-wise. Real best mean must beat the null 95th pct AND clear the FULL longshot spread before significance. This is the exact null the lab's existing gauntlet already runs.
- **Likely failure mode:** (i) financing/cost leak — the ~3-4c gross gap is at-or-below the round-trip longshot spread+gas; charging cost on the full 95-99c NO notional likely erases it. Secondary (c) selection inflation across the band x lead grid and correlated-event N-shrink.
- **$0 kill-shot:** -

**PM02 — Favorite under-pricing (>=95c): is a 96c YES a free 4c, or a rare-loss variance trap?**  
*prior:* worth-testing (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 4/5  
- **Claim:** Markets priced >=0.95 YES at a fixed lead resolve YES MORE often than price implies (the mirror of longshot bias), so buying these near-certain favorites yields positive expectancy net of cost — UNLESS the rare full loss and multi-week collateral financing collapse it.
- **Mechanism:** Favorite-longshot symmetry predicts the favorite side is under-priced because arbitrage capital that would push 96c->100c is tied up (collateral locked to resolution, UMA tail fear), leaving a small persistent discount on near-certain YES.
- **Trade:** At a fixed lead, buy YES on every market with mid in [0.95,1.0); hold to resolution; capture (1-price) when YES, lose price when NO; net of the ~0.1c liquid-favorite spread + financing on the locked $1 notional from entry to resolution.
- **Ground truth:** Resolved label is exact; empirical YES rate in the >=95c bucket vs mean price is a direct binomial calibration test with CIs from resolved counts. The upset rate (e.g. 5/94 observed in a slice) is directly countable.
- **Right null:** The obvious null "Binomial(n, mean_price), is realized-YES > price?" is wrong twice. (1) Direction: the favorite-longshot literature and the hypothesis's own cited slice both say the >=95c favorite is OVER-priced (realized YES rate BELOW price), not under-priced — so the one-sided test must be framed as "can you reject calibration in the FAVORITE's favor net of the half-spread you must cross?", and the breakeven realized-YES rate is not the price but price+h. (2) Multiplicity: the right null is the calibrated-Bernoulli FAMILY-WISE MAX-statistic (resample every market ~ Bernoulli(p_mid), recompute the best mean over the entire searched {direction x band x threshold x lead x volume-floor} grid, take the per-draw max) — exactly the surrogate already coded in calib_gauntlet.ts gate 7. Beating a single-bucket binomial is meaningless; you must beat the max over the whole favorite-slice search, AFTER charging the half-spread and financing on the full $1 collateral over the realized lock.
- **Likely failure mode:** (i) financing/cost leak on full notional over a long collateral lockup + fat-tail variance; the expectancy is asymmetric so DSR @ honest N collapses on rare-loss variance.
- **$0 kill-shot:** One binomial on the >=0.95 resolved bucket: compute realized YES rate vs (mean_price + half_spread). The hypothesis's own cited slice (5/94 NO-upsets = 94.7% realized vs 96% implied) makes the favorite OVER-priced by ~1.3c with one-sided p=0.825 — cannot even reject calibration, let alone clear price+spread. The under-pricing premise is empirically backwards before financing, DSR, or holdout is touched; net-of-spread the strategy loses to blind-NO and to the calibrated-Bernoulli MAX surrogate, dead at the baselines gate. Asymmetry is the coffin: at 96c one NO wipes ~28 wins, so even a hypothetical +2c true under-pricing gives only t~1.5 over 200 trades vs a ~3.3-sigma DSR hurdle at honest N.

**PM03 — Mid-price (0.40-0.60) is the only cost-survivable calibration zone — but power is worst there**  
*prior:* likely-KILL (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 2/5  
- **Claim:** Any net-of-cost calibration edge lives in mid-priced liquid contracts, not the tail: the gap there is smaller in cents but the spread is ~0.1c so the gap/cost ratio clears the hurdle where the tail cannot — yet near 0.5 the per-market Bernoulli variance is maximal, so detecting a 2-3c bias needs the most data of any bucket.
- **Mechanism:** Edge that survives is edge net of the spread hurdle. Tail longshots have a bigger theoretical gap but structurally wide/illiquid spreads and rare-event variance; mid liquid markets have ~0.1c spreads so even a 2-3c systematic calibration error clears the cost gate. But mid-price is also where the book is most arbitraged, so the prior is gap~=0.
- **Trade:** Build the calibration curve restricted to [0.40,0.60] at a fixed lead on liquid (volume-filtered) markets only; if realized YES deviates from price by > 2x measured spread, trade the deviation direction; hold to resolution.
- **Ground truth:** Resolution scores it directly; the [0.40,0.60] bucket binomial calibration vs price is clean and the cost hurdle is the empirically measured spread.
- **Right null:** Obvious null (per-bucket Binomial(n,price)) is wrong because the calibration curve is not one test: lead time, volumeNum cutoff, bucket width, and the "trade if |gap|>k*spread" threshold k are all searched. Right null = family-wise MAX-statistic of the cost-adjusted calibration gap over the grid (lead x vol-cutoff x bucket-width x k), computed on resolution-label permutations within liquid mid-band markets, with the empirically-measured cost hurdle (>=1 tick = 1c half-spread, plus gas) SUBTRACTED before any significance test. Edge must beat the max of that searched-and-cost-netted surrogate, not a single Bernoulli.
- **Likely failure mode:** (h) price-clock / weak-signal: mid-price near 0.5 is where calibration is theoretically TIGHTEST (most arbitraged), so the most likely truth is gap~=0 and any deviation is small-sample noise that fails the block-bootstrap CI.
- **$0 kill-shot:** CLOB tick-size distribution: 968/1000 live markets have minimum_tick_size=0.01, so the minimum representable spread on ~97% of the book is 1 cent, not the claimed "~0.1c". The mechanism's central gap/cost ratio is therefore overstated by ~10x and inverts: a 2-3c calibration gap minus a >=1c half-spread cost floor near p=0.5 (where the prior gap is ~0 because the zone is most arbitraged) leaves no cost-survivable edge. One curl to /markets reading minimum_tick_size falsifies the premise before any calibration math.

**PM04 — Time-to-resolution drift: is the calibration gap monotone in lead time (a datable clock)?**  
*prior:* worth-testing (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 4/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 3/5  
- **Claim:** Favorite-longshot mispricing is largest far from resolution (T-30d) and converges toward calibration by T-1d, so the same contract carries a monotone calibration gap vs lead time and the drift itself is a separable, datable signal rather than static mispricing.
- **Mechanism:** Information arrives and longshot enthusiasm decays as the event nears; smart capital corrects extreme prices late. If true, optimal entry is early (max gap) and the drift toward fair value is the realized PnL — separable from static calibration.
- **Trade:** For each resolved market read YES price at T-30d, T-7d, T-1d; test whether the gap (realized YES - price) is monotone decreasing in proximity; enter at the lead with the largest reliable gap; the drift toward terminal value is the PnL.
- **Ground truth:** Resolution fixes the terminal value at 0/1; the full price path from each lead to terminal is observed, so the gap-vs-lead curve is measurable per bucket.
- **Right null:** The obvious null ("shuffle lead labels within a market and test if the monotone-drift statistic exceeds the shuffled max") is WRONG because it can be beaten by pure mechanics: under perfect calibration price(t) is a MARTINGALE that necessarily fans toward 0/1 as uncertainty resolves, so the gap |realized - price| shrinks monotonically with proximity even when there is zero exploitable edge. That monotonicity is an artifact of variance collapse, not mispricing. The right null is the CALIBRATED-MARTINGALE/Bernoulli surrogate applied to the realized price PATH: for each market draw outcome ~ Bernoulli(price_at_lead) and re-walk; the drift PnL of buying at T-30d and selling at T-1d has E[PnL]=0 by construction (martingale increments). Take the family-wise MAX over the (lead-pair x band x category) grid. Real drift is a signal ONLY if it beats this martingale-max AND is separable from the static parent: the decomposition gap(T-30d)-gap(T-1d) = price(T-1d)-price(T-30d) is identically the buy-and-hold return on a statically-miscalibrated contract, so PM04 is PM01/PM02 with a shorter holding period, not a new claim.
- **Likely failure mode:** (f) no separable premium over the suspect static-calibration parent (PM01/PM02) — if static bias is ~0 net of cost, its time-derivative is also ~0; plus (e) detection latency since the early-lead price often has no executable size.
- **$0 kill-shot:** Run calib_gauntlet.ts at lead=p_7d AND lead=p_1h on the fetched resolved set: if the static net-of-spread mean return is <=0 (or fails the calibrated-Bernoulli MAX surrogate) at both leads, PM04 is instantly dead, because drift PnL = static-gap(early) - static-gap(late) is the difference of two non-edges and you pay the wide longshot half-spread on BOTH the early entry and the exit. The single cheapest number that kills it: the mean of (price(T-1d) - price(T-30d) - 2*halfSpread) over same-token markets where a T-30d print exists — if that mean's block-bootstrap CI includes 0, the datable clock carries no separable signal.

**PM05 — Category-conditioned bias: sports/novelty books more longshot-biased than sharp political books**  
*prior:* likely-KILL (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 4/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 4/5  
- **Claim:** Calibration error in the <=10c bucket is heterogeneous by category — retail/sentiment-dominated categories (sports moneylines, celebrity/novelty) show larger longshot over-pricing than deep, sharp political/macro markets — and the worst category is tradeable where its within-category gap exceeds its own spread.
- **Mechanism:** Sharper, higher-volume political markets attract arbitrage capital that flattens the calibration curve; sports/novelty markets are retail- and sentiment-driven, where probability overweighting persists. Conditioning on category isolates where the bias actually lives.
- **Trade:** Tag resolved markets by event slug/title (Gamma category field is >99% null), build a per-category calibration curve, trade NO-on-longshots only in categories whose measured gap > that category's typical spread.
- **Ground truth:** Resolution gives the per-market label; per-category empirical YES rate vs price is a clean conditional binomial calibration test.
- **Right null:** The obvious null ("the worst category's within-bucket calibration gap is non-zero / its t-stat beats 0") is wrong twice over. (1) It ignores that you selected the worst of K categories: the correct null is the FAMILY-WISE MAX-statistic — pool all resolved <=10c markets, permute the CATEGORY LABELS (preserving each market's price and realized outcome), recompute every category's gap, take the MAX gap across categories per permutation, and require the chosen category's observed gap to beat the 95th pct of that permuted MAX distribution. This is the exact null that flipped Q9 (DSR 0.476@N=96) and O3 (surrogate p=0.093@N=312) to KILL. (2) Even that under-nulls the economics: the right ECONOMIC null is "net-of-spread NO-longshot return in the worst category = a matched random-longshot lottery," because the calibration gap (a few cents) is dwarfed by the longshot spread (measured live at 150-200% of a <=10c price). The category that looks "worst-calibrated" is mechanically the thinnest/widest-spread one (sports novelty), so the cross-category gap and the cross-category spread are the SAME latent variable — illiquidity — not two independent things you can arbitrage one against the other.
- **Likely failure mode:** (c) selection inflation under honest N — reporting the worst category without the across-category MAX-stat penalty is the classic fake-significance (the exact mode that flipped Q9/O3 to KILL in the crypto program).
- **$0 kill-shot:** One query to CLOB /spread on <=10c outcomes (already run): longshot spread is 150-200% of the outcome price, i.e. ~3-9c to cross on a <=10c contract, while the entire longshot-bias calibration gap is a few cents. The hypothesis's own trade gate ("gap > category spread") therefore fires in ZERO categories — net-of-spread the edge is gone before the MAX-stat penalty even applies. The worst-calibrated category is just the widest-spread/thinnest one (gap and spread are both illiquidity), so they cannot be arbitraged against each other. Cost gate binds; KILL.

### Copy-trading & wallet-skill (the viral claim)

**PM16 — Top-PnL wallet skill persistence (TRAIN-rank -> OOS copy at 60s public delay)**  
*prior:* likely-KILL (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 5/5  
- **Claim:** Wallets ranked top-decile by realized PnL over a TRAIN window earn a positive, spread-and-delay-net return when their subsequent OOS trades are mirrored at a realistic 60s public delay; i.e. skill persists across a disjoint time split. This is the literal viral 'copy top wallets' claim.
- **Mechanism:** If a subset of wallets has genuine forecasting/edge skill, their per-market realized PnL should be autocorrelated across disjoint windows (mutual-fund-persistence logic). Polymarket resolves, so each TRAIN-window PnL is ground-truth, not a noisy price proxy.
- **Trade:** Build the leaderboard from data-api positions+trades over TRAIN; take top-N wallets; for every OOS trade simulate entering the same outcome 60s later at the CLOB mid 60s post-timestamp, pay the half-spread, hold to resolution; aggregate net PnL and per-trade Sharpe vs the wallet's own realized PnL and vs random-wallet copying.
- **Ground truth:** Every OOS market resolves, so the copied position payoff is exact; the TRAIN/OOS split makes persistence a clean decidable test.
- **Right null:** The obvious null ("chosen top-decile OOS Sharpe = 0") is wrong twice over: (1) it counts N=1 when the decile was argmax-selected from thousands of wallets, and (2) it ignores that Polymarket PnL leaderboards are mechanically dominated by VOLUME x BASE-RATE, not skill — a wallet that only buys 0.90 favorites books ~90% winrate and large realized PnL by construction with zero edge over the posted price. The right null is a TWO-LAYER surrogate: (a) wallet-label-shuffle that preserves every wallet's actual trade timing, entry odds, side, and size but randomly reassigns the TRAIN rank, then takes the FAMILY-WISE MAX top-decile OOS Sharpe across many shuffles (the §5.3 machinery that already flipped BTC-netflow and O3-NVT to KILL); plus (b) an odds/side-stratified null that resamples synthetic wallets matching the real top-decile's entry-probability and notional distribution, so any tilt that is just 'they held favorites into resolution' is reproduced by the surrogate. Real top-decile OOS Sharpe must clear the family-wise 95th pct of BOTH. Add a calendar-reanchor (random split dates) so persistence is not an artifact of one favorable split.
- **Likely failure mode:** (c) selection inflation under honest N / single-config surrogate — top-PnL is dominated by luck+volume on a high-base-rate population; the wallet-label-shuffle reproduces most of the OOS tilt and family-wise MAX kills the chosen decile.
- **$0 kill-shot:** TRAIN->OOS Spearman rank-correlation of per-wallet realized PnL/Sharpe (plus top-decile transition matrix) on a single disjoint date split — one pass over data-api/trades + Gamma labels, no CLOB pulls. Expect Spearman near 0 / regression-to-the-mean; if so, persistence is dead before spread or delay ever enter.

**PM17 — The 60s public-delay tax is the h=0 tautology: copyable wallet alpha = 0**  
*prior:* likely-KILL (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 5/5  
- **Claim:** Even for wallets with a statistically real edge at THEIR fill price, mirroring at the mandatory >=60s public delay erases it: the informative trade moves the CLOB mid within the minute (price impact + others copying), so the copier enters at a materially worse price. The 'mirror with 60s delay = print money' claim fails on the delay leg alone.
- **Mechanism:** Copy-trading is structurally an h>=1 lagged signal — the leader's trade is public only after execution. If the leader has information, the mid re-prices toward fair during the delay; the copier captures the post-impact, worse-priced residual. This is the order-flow h=0 tautology ported to prediction markets: alpha lives in the contemporaneous fill, the lagged leg is ~0.
- **Trade:** For wallets that show real edge at delay=0 (pass PM16 at 0s), recompute OOS copy-PnL at delay {0s,60s,300s,900s} using CLOB mid at each offset; measure the edge-decay curve; report the lagged-leg (60s) Sharpe alone against the gates.
- **Ground truth:** The price 60s after each leader trade and the final resolution are both exact, so the delay-induced PnL loss per trade is a measured quantity; the decay curve is fully decidable.
- **Right null:** The proposed null (random-nearby-minute calendar-shuffle entry) is the WRONG null: it only shows the delayed copy isn't a coin flip vs. arbitrary entry timing — a trivially-passable bar that proves nothing about copyable alpha. Two correct nulls are needed. (1) For the DECAY claim ("alpha erased by 60s"): the per-trade PAIRED contrast Sharpe(delay=0, leader fill) minus Sharpe(delay=60s), bootstrapped over the SAME trade set — the obvious null wrongly compares 60s to a random entry instead of to the only price that ever printed alpha (the 0s fill). (2) For the residual-alpha claim ("the 60s leg is itself ~0"): the lagged-leg copy return must beat the WALLET-LABEL-SHUFFLE family-wise MAX null at delay=60s (copy random eligible wallets, also entering +60s), exactly as the PM16 parent does — because a positive 60s mean could just be longshot drift / market-wide YES-resolution skew shared by random wallets, not leader skill. Random-timestamp entry doesn't strip that shared drift; wallet-shuffle does.
- **Likely failure mode:** (b) the h=0 order-flow tautology — edge lives in the contemporaneous bar; the strictly-lagged (>=60s public) leg is ~0. Reinforced by (g) reverse-causality (their trade moved the price you copy into).
- **$0 kill-shot:** Run the EXISTING copy_trading_gauntlet.ts (PM16) and read one line: [gate surrogate] wallet-label-shuffle. If real-best copy mean does not beat null95 (random wallets from the same pool, same OOS, hold-to-resolution), copyable alpha is 0 at delay=0 — so the 60s-delayed leg is strictly worse than zero and PM17 is dead before a single prices-history call. This is the cheapest because the trade tape is already cached and the surrogate is already coded; it kills both PM16 and PM17 in one pass without any windowed +60s mid fetches.

**PM18 — Winrate-ranked wallets are NOT profit-ranked: the '70%+ winrate, 47 traders' favorite-loading artifact**  
*prior:* likely-KILL (critique: likely-KILL) · *provability* 5/5 · *edge* 2/5 · *directness* 5/5  
- **Claim:** Wallets selected for high TRAIN winrate (the literal '47 traders with 70%+ winrate' viral claim) have OOS copy-PnL indistinguishable from zero (or negative net of spread), because high winrate is mechanically produced by buying near-certain favorites (0.85-0.97) where winrate is high but EV-per-dollar is thin and spread/upset-tail eats it.
- **Mechanism:** Winrate != profit: a wallet that only buys YES at 0.92 wins ~92% by construction with near-zero edge. The viral posts conflate winrate with skill. Entry prices are observable, so the winrate can be proven a price-level artifact, not forecasting skill.
- **Trade:** Rank wallets by TRAIN winrate; OOS-copy at 60s delay to resolution; regress OOS net-PnL on the wallet's mean entry price; show winrate-top wallets cluster at high entry prices and their delay-and-spread-net OOS edge is ~0; contrast against PnL-ranked (PM16) wallets.
- **Ground truth:** Winrate and realized profit are both exactly computable from resolved markets; the dissociation (high winrate, ~0 net profit) is directly measurable, falsifying the viral 'winrate=skill' claim with ground-truth numbers.
- **Right null:** The obvious null ("winrate-top wallets have OOS edge = 0") is wrong because it lets a trivially-winning favorite-loader masquerade as the alternative: ANY wallet buying YES at 0.92 "beats zero winrate" and even has slightly positive raw EV in a calibrated market, so a naive null is too easy to reject for the wrong reason. The RIGHT null is the FAVORITE-LOADING PLACEBO + family-wise MAX: build synthetic zero-skill wallets that, per trade, BUY the favorite at the prevailing CLOB mid (same entry-price distribution and the same market set as the real winrate-top cohort), then apply the identical 60s-delay + half-spread + gas haircut. The real cohort must beat the MAX of this placebo's net-PnL distribution across the entire (winrate-threshold x window x k x delay) grid — not beat zero, and NOT beat on winrate (winrate is mechanically ~mean-entry-price and is not a skill statistic at all). Equivalently: the surrogate is "rank wallets by mean entry price" (a pure risk-profile sort); winrate-top must separate from price-sorted placebo on net-PnL.
- **Likely failure mode:** (d) de-risking masquerading as timing — de-risking via favorite-loading masquerades as skill; high winrate is a risk-profile artifact, not edge.
- **$0 kill-shot:** The single cheapest kill: regress (or just scatter) TRAIN winrate vs TRAIN mean-entry-price across eligible wallets from the already-cached trade tape. If corr ≈ +0.85+ (it will be — winrate is near-deterministic in entry price for resolved binaries), winrate is proven a price-level artifact, the "70%+/47-traders" ranking is a favorite-loading sort, and any OOS net-PnL > 0 is then explained by raw favorite EV minus spread (which on 0.85-0.97 longshorts the wide ask eats), not skill — dead before the bootstrap. One Pearson correlation on free data ends it.
